# Carpe Diem — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** [DATE — e.g. 9 May 2026]

**Operator:** [YOUR LEGAL NAME OR BUSINESS NAME] (“we”, “us”).

**Contact:** [YOUR CONTACT EMAIL]

This policy describes how Carpe Diem (“the app”) handles information when you use it on your device. By using the app, you agree to this policy alongside the store terms that apply to you (Apple App Store or Google Play).

---

## Summary

Carpe Diem is **local-first**: your wishes, pockets, and most planning data are stored **on your device** (SQLite). We do **not** operate a Carpe Diem account server that stores your wish text in the cloud.

Some features send limited data to **third-party services** (see below), for example when you search for places, view maps, subscribe, or when the app reports technical errors.

---

## 1. Information stored on your device

The app stores content you create or edit, including wish titles, notes, planning fields, and (if you use them) linked place names, addresses, coordinates, and related identifiers, **locally on your device**. This data remains under your control unless you back up your device through Apple or Google’s normal device backup mechanisms.

---

## 2. In-app purchases and subscriptions

If you purchase **Carpe Diem Pro** (or any subscription we offer):

- **Apple** or **Google** processes payment. They receive payment and account identifiers as described in their privacy policies.
- We use **RevenueCat** to validate subscriptions and sync **entitlement state** (for example whether your subscription is active). RevenueCat receives **transaction-related data** from the stores (such as product identifiers, subscription status, and anonymous or app-generated customer IDs), **not** your wish list text.  
  RevenueCat’s privacy policy: [https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy](https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy)

---

## 3. Location, maps, and places

If you use **location**, **maps**, or **place search**:

- **Google** (Maps SDK, Places API, and related services) may process queries and location-related data according to **Google’s** policies and your Google account / device settings.
- Data sent is what is needed to show maps, search, or resolve a place you choose (for example search text or coordinates you trigger through the app).

Google’s privacy information: [https://policies.google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)

---

## 4. Diagnostics and crash reporting (optional)

If enabled in your build, the app may use **Sentry** (or similar tools) to collect **crash reports** and technical diagnostics (for example stack traces, device type, app version, and breadcrumbs you configure in code). This helps fix bugs. It is **not** intended to collect your wish titles or notes as product analytics; avoid entering highly sensitive secrets into the app if you are concerned about crash log context in principle.

Sentry’s privacy policy: [https://sentry.io/privacy/](https://sentry.io/privacy/)

---

## 5. Notifications

If you turn on **reminder notifications**, scheduling is handled using the **operating system’s** notification APIs on your device. We do not need a separate cloud account to send you those local reminders.

---

## 6. Children

The app is **not directed at children under 13** (or the minimum age required in your region). Do not use the app if you are below that age, or ensure a parent or guardian supervises use as required by local law.

---

## 7. International users and your rights

Depending on where you live (for example the **UK** or **EEA**), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing of personal data held by **us** (for example if you email us with a support request that identifies you).

Processors such as **Apple**, **Google**, and **RevenueCat** are primarily responsible for their own processing; you may exercise rights with them via their privacy settings and policies where applicable.

---

## 8. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The **“Last updated”** date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy, unless applicable law requires otherwise.
